# CEO Succession Timing (relative to last 0→1)

## Definition

A non-founder CEO's strategic runway is largely determined by *how recently the founder shipped the company's last 0→1 product*. The closer the handoff is to a fresh 0→1, the more room the successor has to scale that invention 1→n and look brilliant in the process. The longer the founder stuck around past their last 0→1, the more the successor inherits a company already past its peak, with no choice but to manage a decline.

In Ben's words from the article:

> The challenge for CEOs following iconic founders is that the person who took the company from 0 to 1 usually sticks around for 2, 3, 4, etc.; by the time they step down the only way forward is often down. Jobs, however, by virtue of leaving the world too soon, left Apple only a few years after its most important 0 to 1 product ever, meaning it was Cook who was in charge of growing and expanding Apple's most revolutionary device yet.
