# Zero to One

## Definition

Peter Thiel's distinction between two kinds of progress:

- **Vertical / 0→1 progress** — doing something nobody has done before. Inventing the typewriter where there was none.
- **Horizontal / 1→n progress** — copying what works, scaling it out. Building 100 typewriters once you have one.

The point of the framework is that 0→1 work is structurally different from 1→n work, both in difficulty (it's harder to imagine because no template exists) and in who is good at it.

## Origin

Peter Thiel, *Zero to One* (2014). Ben Thompson uses it as a recurring lens for evaluating products, founders, and CEO succession.

## Examples across articles

- [2026-05-04 Tim Cook's Impeccable Timing](../articles/2026-05-04-tim-cooks-impeccable-timing-stratechery-by-ben-thompson.md) — Ben uses it to frame the Jobs vs. Cook divide. Jobs made 0→1 products (Mac, iPod, iPhone, iPad — and Apple itself). Cook scaled them 1→n. Vision Pro is dismissed as "more of a 0.5" — i.e. not really new.
